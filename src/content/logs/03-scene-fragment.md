The corridor remembered footsteps it should not have held. Dust lay in a pattern too deliberate to be wind—more like someone had swept once, badly, then abandoned the task when the sound in the walls changed pitch.

She stopped where the paint blistered and listened for the secondary hum that meant the grid was awake.

Nothing. That was sometimes worse.
